Pro Strategy

Finding Your Niche

Don't be a generalist. Be a specialist.

The Jack-of-All-Trades Trap

If you upload an FPS compilation on Monday, an RPG walkthrough on Wednesday, and a tech review on Friday, the Instagram algorithm gets confused. It won't know who to push your content to, and your viewers won't know why they should follow you. To blow up fast, you must pick one specific lane and absolutely dominate it.

The Creator Expansion Roadmap

How to scale from a single game to a massive personal brand.

1

The Specialist

Hyper-Niche: Focus on one game (e.g., God of War cinematic edits). You become the undeniable authority for that specific fanbase. Growth is fast.

2

The Genre Master

Broadening: Once you hit 10k-50k followers, expand to similar games in that genre (e.g., Ghost of Tsushima, Elden Ring). The audience transfers easily.

3

The Personality

Mass Appeal: People are now following you for you, not just the game. You can now post PC builds, setup tours, and play whatever you want.

How to Choose Your Starting Niche

Look at what you naturally play and what has a visual "wow" factor. Story-driven games with incredible graphics (like Red Dead Redemption 2 or Black Myth: Wukong) are perfect for cinematic editors. Competitive games (like Valorant or CS2) are better for high-IQ gameplay breakdowns. Find where your editing skills meet a passionate fanbase.
