Performance Data

Real-world testing for rendering edits and 1440p/4K gaming.

Budget Gaming Tier
(Ryzen 5 7600 / RTX 4060)

~₹75k - ₹80k

This is the perfect starting point. The Ryzen 5 7600 paired with an RTX 4060 provides enough headroom for solid 1080p gaming and handles 1080p/1440p video editing timelines effortlessly.

1080p Gaming (Ultra Settings) ~90 FPS
90 FPS
Premiere Pro 4K Render (10 Min Cinematic) ~6 mins
6:15

Pros

  • Excellent 1080p performance for competitive titles and action games.
  • Highly power-efficient; doesn't require an expensive power supply.
  • Great entry-level hardware encoding (NVENC) for streaming and recording.

Cons

  • Struggles with heavy, multi-layered 4K timelines without using proxies.
  • 8GB VRAM limit means newer unoptimized games might require turning down textures.

Pro Creator Tier
(i9-14900K / RTX 4090)

~₹2.6L - ₹3.5L

Built for the endgame. Whether you are running a triple-monitor setup or rendering dense After Effects compositions, this machine does not bottleneck.

4K Gaming (Ultra + RayTracing) ~140+ FPS
140+ FPS
Premiere Pro 4K Render (10 Min Cinematic) ~1.5 mins
1:30

Pros

  • Shreds 4K/8K video timelines seamlessly.
  • Massive 24GB VRAM is entirely future-proof for heavy modding and rendering.
  • Top-tier dual AV1 encoders for flawless, high-bitrate streaming quality.

Cons

  • Requires a massive budget.
  • Very high power draw (requires a 1000W+ Platinum PSU).
  • High heat output demands a premium AIO liquid cooler and a high-airflow case.
